What is e-waste?

E-waste, or electronic waste, is any discarded electrical or electronic device. This includes everything from computers and laptops to cell phones and TVs.

Why is it important to recycle e-waste?

E-waste contains a number of hazardous materials, such as lead, mercury, and cadmium. These materials can leach into the soil and water if e-waste is not recycled properly. This can pose a serious threat to human health and the environment.

The effects of e-waste on humans and the environment

Exposure to e-waste can cause a number of health problems, including cancer, neurological disorders, and reproductive problems. E-waste also pollutes the environment, contaminating the soil, water, and air.

How to find an e-waste facility near you

There are a number of ways to find an e-waste facility near you. You can search online, contact your local government, or ask at electronics stores and other businesses.

Tips for recycling e-waste responsibly

Here are a few tips for recycling e-waste responsibly:

  • Find a certified e-waste recycling facility.
  • Remove all personal data from your devices before recycling them.
  • Recycle batteries separately from other electronics.
  • Do not mix different types of electronics together.
